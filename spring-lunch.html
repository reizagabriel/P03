Spring Lunch

Created social media posts for a fundraising event.

The Skills

Digital Marketing, Copywriting, Social Media

The Project

As a volunteer, I worked with the Marketing Manager on a social media campaign promoting a gala fundraiser. The event was hosted by Big Sisters, a non-profit organization that offers mentorship programs for young girls.

My role was to write posts for Facebook, Instagram, and Twitter.

The Strategy

To keep posts cohesive, I followed established marketing elements. The cornerstone of the campaign is an image of a young girl and the slogan “You don’t need wings to fly.” Elements of flight represent one’s rise to their potential.

I also followed the page's optimal times for posting: 11 a.m. and 2 p.m. This is based on previous data gathered from Facebook and Instagram Insights and Twitter Analytics.

The Words

I wrote posts for Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram. I also suggested accompanying images and ideal times for posting based on data from analytics.

When writing, I followed the brand’s calm yet uplifting tone and concise sentences. To sound personable, I used pronouns like ‘our’ and ‘your'. For consistency, each post featured the event hashtag #SpringLunch2017. To increase engagement, I also ended each post with a call-to-action such as a link to register or to read more information about the event.

The Numbers

The fundraiser was a success, raising over $79,000.

The posts themselves also directed traffic to the profiles according to Facebook and Instagram Insights and Twitter Analytics. On Facebook, they gathered over 1,000 impressions—among the highest for February 2017.
