Alice in Wonderland

Designed the layout of a mini book.

The Skill

Print design

The Project

For a publishing course, I designed the layout for the written content provided. The story was a portion of Lewis Caroll’s Alice’s Adventures in Wonderland. Pages were printed and hand-bound to create a mini book.

The Branding

I began by developing a visual style for the project. I envisioned a minimalistic look that ‘sells’ a children’s story to adults. I used plenty of white space, outlines of characters as icons, and a tri-colour palette. This is embodied by the book cover:

It also imitates the look of playing cards—a reference to a scene in the story. The red, white, and black colour palette achieves this look. The ‘A’ refers to the Ace of Hearts, but also ‘A’ for ‘Alice’.

The Layout

The inside pages follow the layout of a standard book. The serif font is readable on print and follows the convention of using serifs in books.

To break monotony, I styled the beginning and end of each chapter. I used style guide elements like white space, flat icons, and a red, white, and black colour scheme.

The Results

The design put a modern twist on a classic children’s story. It received an A- grade and the instructor commended its clarity and consistent branding.

The Tools

Adobe InDesign, Adobe Illustrator, Adobe Photoshop
