Write articles for an online magazine.

The Skill

Editorial writing

The Publication

Her Campus is an online magazine that aims to empower college women. As a writer for the SFU chapter, I contribute articles on a biweekly basis.

The Pitches

When brainstorming topics, I ask two things: What are my knowledge and interests? Which ones are most relevant to my audience at this time? I answer the latter by observing fellow college women, tuning into current events, and researching similar publications. Due to my breadth of interests, my articles range from fashion and humour, to lifestyle and social issues.

Before writing, I propose these topics to the Senior Editor, a fellow member of the Her Campus at SFU team. Each pitch includes a title and short description. After feedback and approval from Editor, I proceed to writing.

The Voice

When writing, I maintain the casual yet uplifting tone of the Her Campus brand. Articles aim to serve the reader by providing tips, sharing a new perspective, or simply providing a good laugh.

I have also developed a distinct voice—one that is clear, catchy, and creative. Many of my articles are whimsical and playful in tone, aiming to amuse the reader. I incorporate humour through GIFs and wordplay like puns and alliterations. Recently, I have experimented with a serious, contemplative style when writing about social issues.
